# Product Context
_Last updated: 2026-03-01_

## Vision
The project tracker that gets out of your way - fast to set up, easy to report from, built for engineers not managers.

## OKRs / current quarter goals
- **Activation:** 60% of new teams complete their first project within 7 days of signup (currently 41%)
- **Retention:** Reduce 30-day churn from 28% to 20%
- **Expansion:** Launch one feature that drives team-size upgrades (currently avg 4.2 seats)

## Personas

### Sam - Engineering Lead
- **Who:** Lead engineer at a 10-person startup, reports to CTO
- **Goal:** Keep the team aligned without spending time in meetings
- **Pain:** Stakeholders keep asking for status updates in Slack; no easy way to share progress without logging into the tool

### Alex - Founder / Stakeholder
- **Who:** Non-technical co-founder, checks in on engineering progress weekly
- **Goal:** Know what's shipping and when, without needing to learn a new tool
- **Pain:** Has to ask Sam for updates; doesn't want a Trackr login just to see a status report

## Positioning
Trackr is for small engineering teams who want lightweight tracking without the overhead of Jira or Linear. It's faster to set up, cheaper, and designed for teams where the PM and the engineer are often the same person.

## Roadmap context
This quarter: CSV export, public status pages, Slack notifications. Next quarter: time tracking improvements, GitHub PR linking. Not on the roadmap: Gantt charts, resource planning, enterprise SSO.

## Channels
- In-app onboarding emails (Resend)
- Product changelog (in-app + email digest)
- Twitter/X (@trackrhq, 1.2K followers)
- Small Discord community (180 members)

## Competitors
- **Linear** - more powerful, more complex, targets larger teams. We win on simplicity and price.
- **Notion** - flexible but not purpose-built for tracking. We win on structure and reporting.
- **Jira** - enterprise-focused, heavy. We win on setup time and UX.
